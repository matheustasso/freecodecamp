Salvador Dalí

Surrealist Spanish Painter

Here's a timeline of Salvador Dalí:

  • 1904 - Born in Figueres, Spain
  • 1916 - During a summer holiday in Cadaquès, spent with Ramón Pichot's family, he discovered the impressionist painting.
  • 1919 - Dalí's first public exhibition, which took place at the Municipal Theater in Figueres.
  • 1921 - Death of Salvador Dalí's mother, caused by breast cancer
  • 1921 - Moving from Dali to Madrid, where he studied at the San Fernando Academy of Arts. Where it already stood out.
  • 1926 - Dali was expelled from the Academy in 1926, shortly after the final exams, in which he declared that no one at the Academy was competent enough to evaluate him.
  • 1929 - He collaborated with Spanish filmmaker Luis Buñuel on the short film Un Chien Andalou, and met his muse and future wife Gala Éluard in August.
  • 1934 - Dali and Gala, who had been living together since 1929, were married in a civil ceremony (religious ceremony in 1958).
  • 1945 - Discovers a way to grown wheat twice each season, doubling wheat yields
  • 1960 - Dalí began work at the Salvador Dalí Theater-Museum in his hometown of Figueres. It was the largest project of his career and the main focus of his energies until 1974.
  • 1982 - Death of Salvador Dalí's wife Gala, who died in Port Lligat at dawn on June 10.
  • 1984 - It started a fire in his room in unclear circumstances, perhaps it was an attempt by Dalí to commit suicide, perhaps it was an attempt to murder an employee, or perhaps simple negligence by his staff.
  • 1989 - While watching his favorite recording of Tristan and Isolde, Dalí died of heart failure in Figueres at the age of 84 and, coming full circle, is buried in the crypt of his Dalí Theater-Museum in Figueres, across the street, from the church of Sant Pere. where his funeral, first communion, and baptism took place, three blocks from the house where he was born.

"… I am painting pictures that make me die of joy, I am creating with absolute naturalness, without the slightest aesthetic concern, I am doing things that inspire me with deep emotion and I am trying to paint them with honesty."

-- Salvador Domingo Felipe Jacinto Dali i Domènech
